01 / NEW SYNOPSIS

A live counter archive

Sampling is a choreographic solo in which the body operates as a living archive, screen, sensor and sampler. Flamenco footwork, breaking, football, popular culture, family memory and digital images pass through a body that cuts, repeats, delays, distorts and recomposes them.

The work begins while the audience enters. A rhythmic structure holds the room as the performer prepares cables, cameras, shoes, microphones, reflective surfaces and a football. The stage gradually becomes a recording and projection device. Each action creates an image, each image returns to the body, and each return changes the movement.

Four projections create a frontal field of multiplied images. The body receives them through costume, skin, mylar and scenic surfaces. The performer controls the apparatus, then enters its delays, residues and visual errors. Choreography, image, sound and light continuously rewrite one another.

How can a solo become a personal voice while acknowledging that every gesture has travelled through other bodies?

04 / RESEARCH METHOD

Three connected laboratories

The laboratories operate as rehearsal tools, public research formats and encounters with local communities.

LAB A

Acoustic body

The floor becomes an instrument and the body becomes a sound generator. Flamenco footwork meets breaking through feet, hands, knees, back, breath, fall and drag.

  • Expanded compas
  • Impact and stumble
  • Floor as resonant surface
LAB B

Body screen

Camera, four projections, mylar, costume, light and delay turn the body into a surface of inscription and return. The stage produces its own archive as the performance unfolds.

  • Camera body
  • Image feedback
  • Delay and residue
LAB C

Ida y vuelta dances

Gestures travel through flamenco, breaking, football, hip hop, television, family memory and national imagery. The performer cuts, delays, exhausts and recomposes them.

  • Mixed authorship
  • Dribble and footwork
  • Archive in movement

05 / TECHNICAL DEVICE

Frontal black box. Four synchronised projections.

BLACK BOX / STAGE
P1P2P3P4 BODY + MYLAR + COSTUME SCREEN
FRONTAL AUDIENCE

The visual device uses four projectors fed from a central video source through a 1 x 4 splitter. The shared signal creates a synchronised frontal image field across body and scenic surfaces.

COMPUTER / LIVE CAMERA ↓ VIDEO PROCESSOR ↓ 1 x 4 HDMI SPLITTER ↓ FOUR PROJECTORS ↓ BODY + SCENIC SURFACES

Space

Black box, controlled darkness, end on audience, dance floor and stable projection positions.

Image

Four compatible projectors, 1 x 4 splitter, computer, camera input, HDMI distribution and mylar.

Sound

Ableton Live, Push 2, Zoom H6, contact microphones, voice microphones and venue sound system.

Light

Light controls image visibility and returns the physical space to full presence.

Adrian Vega works professionally as a lighting technician. He can previsualise, program and adapt the lighting language from inside the creation process. Projector lenses, brightness, throw distances and exact positions will be defined during technical residency.

06 / MEDIATION

Launched with the piece from the beginning

Mediation develops in parallel with the stage work. Each residency can activate a local version of the laboratories with young people, artists, neighbourhood groups or students.

These encounters investigate how gestures travel, how identity is physically learned and how phones, family archives, sport, dance and popular culture shape bodily memory. Questions and materials produced locally return to the rehearsal room.

  • Body as archiveGestures learned through family, school, sport and screens.
  • Sample a gestureRecord, repeat, displace and recompose movement.
  • Camera bodyImage delay, projection and the body as screen.
  • Open studioPublic rehearsal and conversation in each territory.
